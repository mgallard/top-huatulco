Huatulco Packing List

What to pack for Huatulco's sun, boats, markets, dry walks, remote beaches, and airport-to-hotel logistics without overpacking resort extras.

Quick verdict

Pack for sun, salt, heat, and short practical moves between bays. The useful items are not fancy: reef-safe sun protection, sandals plus walking shoes, dry bag, light layers, cash system, and backup swimwear.

Common mistake

The common mistake is underestimating sun exposure on boat days. Shade may be limited, and water reflection makes a casual swim day feel much stronger.

What to do first

Step-by-step

  • Pack one boat-day kit: dry bag, sun shirt, hat, water, motion-sickness help if needed, and cash.
  • Pack one town/culture kit: comfortable shoes, light shirt, small cash, and refillable water.
  • Bring more sun protection than your resort shop budget wants you to buy.
  • Leave room for market purchases if La Crucecita is on your list.

Same-week check

Before you lock this in, check current conditions, operating days, transfer details, and payment expectations. Huatulco planning is easiest when the changeable details are checked close to the date.

How this works in Huatulco

Huatulco days often combine beach, taxi, town, and dinner. Clothes that work only for a resort pool are less useful than light pieces you can wear from boat to lunch to La Crucecita. Copalita and market walks need better footwear than flip-flops.
