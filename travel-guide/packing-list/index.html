Packing guide

Huatulco Packing List

Pack for strong sun, boat spray, uneven town walking, cash-based beach stops, and the difference between resort days and remote bay days.

Quick verdict

What to do first

Best move: pack one small day kit that works for boat, beach, and town. Huatulco days often combine all three.

Practical rules

  • Reef-conscious sun protection, hat, sunglasses, rash guard, and water bottle matter more than extra outfits.
  • Bring sandals for beach days and real walking shoes for Copalita, town wandering, or rocky access.
  • Dry bag or waterproof pouch helps on boat tours and snorkeling mornings.
  • Small first-aid, motion-sickness support, insect repellent, and copies of key documents are practical, not overkill.

Choose this approach if

You want a low-stress trip where transport, money, weather, and safety decisions are handled before they become problems.

Avoid this mistake

Do not plan Huatulco as if every bay, town, and attraction has the same access, services, and return options.
