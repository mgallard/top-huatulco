Bahía Chahue

The town beach of Huatulco. Walkable from La Crucecita restaurants and hotels. Good for evenings, sunsets, and mixing beach time with town life.

Access from town

10-minute flat walk from the main square in La Crucecita. Follow the signs or the locals heading to the beach at sunset.

Taxis from anywhere in La Crucecita or Santa Cruz cost $4–6 USD.

Free public parking at the north end near the small marina.

Beach conditions

Fine sand, gentle slope, usually calm. Some days small waves near the point. Popular with local families on weekends. Lifeguards on duty in high season.

Best for: late afternoon and sunset. The light on the water is excellent and the restaurants behind the beach start to fill up.

Food and drink

Palapas and small restaurants line the back of the beach. Fresh ceviche, grilled fish, and micheladas are the local standard. Prices are lower than the resort beaches.

For more options, walk 5 minutes back into La Crucecita for Oaxacan food and mezcal bars.

Quick facts
  • Water: usually calm
  • Best time: 4 pm – sunset
  • Facilities: restaurants, showers, parking
  • Crowds: local on weekends
  • Shade: palapas available for rent
Tips
  • Bring small bills for beach vendors
  • Stay until after sunset — the colors are worth it
  • Combine with dinner in La Crucecita
  • Watch for occasional strong undertow near the south point
