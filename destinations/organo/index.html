Órgano

The easternmost bay. Boat-only with beautiful beaches and good snorkeling. Quiet and scenic with minimal facilities.

How to get here Things to do

Why visit Órgano

Easternmost bay
Farthest from the main developed area — very quiet.
Multiple beaches
Several small beaches within one bay to explore.
Full-day escape
Ideal for a peaceful, low-key boat day.

How to get there

Boat from Santa Cruz: 25–35 minutes depending on stops. Shared tours $25–40 USD round-trip.

Usually visited on full-day multi-bay tours.

Things to do

  • Explore the different small beaches
  • Snorkel the rocky areas
  • Enjoy the quiet, natural setting
  • Relax with minimal crowds

Practical visitor information

Best time: Morning for clearest water and calmest conditions.

Facilities: Small palapa restaurants on the main beach. Bring water and gear.

Safety: Calm protected water. Standard boat precautions apply.

Quick facts
  • Water: calm, snorkel-friendly
  • Shade: limited
  • Facilities: basic palapas
  • Crowds: low
  • Best months: Nov–Apr
Tips
  • Perfect for a full-day boat excursion
  • Snorkel gear recommended
  • Least crowded of the nine bays
  • Bring cash and water

FAQ

Is Órgano the quietest bay?

Yes — it is the farthest and sees the fewest visitors of all nine bays.

How many beaches are in Órgano?

Several small beaches. Most tours visit the main ones depending on time.
