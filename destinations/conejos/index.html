Huatulco bay guide

Conejos

Conejos travel guide for Huatulco: access, beach conditions, best time, snorkeling, safety, nearby food, and practical visitor tips.

Quick verdict

Conejos is best for quiet resort edge, rocky viewpoints. Smaller bay with upscale lodging nearby, rocky edges, and calm pockets for swimming.

How can I get there easily?

Taxi from Tangolunda area or hotel access depending on where you stay.

For a normal tourist, taxi is usually the simplest choice unless your hotel is already on this bay. Confirm the fare before getting in and carry pesos.

What will I find nearby?

Use this bay as part of a bigger day: La Crucecita for food and errands, Santa Cruz for boats, Chahué for sunset, or Tangolunda for resort-style swimming.

Water, weather, and timing

Morning for water clarity. In dry season, visibility is usually better for snorkeling. In rainy season, mornings still work, but afternoon showers and humidity are more likely.

Practical tips and things to take into account

Public access can be less obvious. Ask your hotel or taxi driver for the easiest entry.

  • Bring water, hat, reef-safe sunscreen, and small bills.
  • Ask about currents before swimming near rocky ends or outside the protected swimming area.
  • Do not stand on coral or chase marine life when snorkeling.

Sources and live checks

Use official Huatulco National Park / CONANP context for protected-area rules, your hotel or boat captain for same-week sea state, and local taxi stands for current fares.
