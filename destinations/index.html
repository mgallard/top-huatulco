Bay planning

Quick verdict: pick the bay by the day you want

The nine bays are close on a map but not interchangeable. Start with the visitor problem: easy resort day, marina/boat logistics, calm seafood lunch, remote scenery, snorkel conditions, or quiet viewpoint.

Choose / avoid

  • Choose this when: you compare access, services, swimming, food, and return logistics before choosing the bay.
  • Avoid this when: you treat remote boat-access bays as simple taxi beach days or assume every bay is safe/swimmable in the same conditions.
  • Better backup idea: If the water day weakens, use Santa Cruz for a lighter harbor meal, La Crucecita for town time, or Chahué for an easy sunset instead of overcommitting.

Compare the bays

Pick the bay that fits your day

On phones, this comparison becomes stacked cards so you can read each bay without pinching or sideways scrolling. On wider screens, use the full table.

Tangolunda

Best for
First-timers, families, resort beach days
How to get there
Taxi from HUX 20–25 min; from La Crucecita 10 min. Best by taxi or hotel shuttle.
Best time
Morning to early afternoon. Wind can add chop later.

Chahué

Best for
Sunsets, local meals, easy evenings
How to get there
Walk 10–15 min from La Crucecita or short taxi from Santa Cruz.
Best time
Late afternoon and sunset.

Santa Cruz

Best for
Boat tours, seafood, cruise arrivals
How to get there
Taxi or walk from La Crucecita; boats leave from the pier.
Best time
Morning for boats; early evening for food.

El Maguey

Best for
Calm water, casual lunch, short boat trips
How to get there
Taxi/road access from town or boat stop from Santa Cruz.
Best time
Morning before tour boats stack up.

Órgano

Best for
Low-key swimming, fewer vendors
How to get there
Usually reached by boat as part of a bay tour.
Best time
Morning boat stop.

Cacaluta

Best for
Scenery, clear water, boat-day feel
How to get there
Boat access is the normal visitor route; hiking access requires local guidance and heat planning.
Best time
Dry-season mornings.

Chachacual

Best for
Snorkeling, clear water, boat tours
How to get there
Boat only. Usually included on multi-bay tours from Santa Cruz.
Best time
Morning light and calm seas.

San Agustín

Best for
Beach walking, local weekends, relaxed lunch
How to get there
Road access is slower and bumpier; boat access depends on sea state.
Best time
Weekdays for quiet; weekends for local energy.

Conejos

Best for
Quiet resort edge, rocky viewpoints
How to get there
Taxi from Tangolunda area or hotel access depending on where you stay.
Best time
Morning for water clarity.
