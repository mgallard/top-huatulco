San Agustín

Longer beach popular with locals on weekends. Some waves, good walking, and snorkeling spots. More natural feel than the developed bays.

How to get here Things to do

Why visit San Agustín

Longer beach
Great for walking and exploring. Less crowded on weekdays.
Local vibe
Popular with Oaxacan families on weekends. Authentic atmosphere.
Mixed conditions
Protected areas stay calm while outer edges have some waves.

How to get there

Dirt road from Copalita or boat from Santa Cruz (20–25 minutes).

Taxi from La Crucecita: $15–20 USD one way, 25–30 minutes.

Colectivo or local bus possible but slow; most visitors take taxi or tour.

Things to do

  • Walk the long stretch of sand
  • Snorkel the rocky points at the ends
  • Enjoy beachside seafood at the palapas
  • People-watch on busy local weekends

Practical visitor information

Best time: Weekdays for fewer people; early morning or late afternoon any day.

Facilities: Basic palapa restaurants and restrooms. Limited shade in some areas.

Safety: Some waves at the outer edges — swim in the more protected central area on windy days.

Quick facts
  • Water: mixed, some waves
  • Shade: palapas available
  • Facilities: basic restaurants
  • Crowds: low weekdays, high weekends
  • Best months: Nov–Apr
Tips
  • Go mid-week to avoid crowds
  • Bring reef-safe sunscreen
  • Good for long walks
  • Combine with a boat tour for easier access

FAQ

Is San Agustín good for swimming?

The central and inner areas are calm enough for swimming. The outer edges can have waves — check conditions before entering.

How crowded does it get on weekends?

Very popular with local families. It can feel busy on Sunday afternoons but is usually manageable.

Is there road access?

Yes, via a dirt road from Copalita, though most visitors arrive by boat or taxi.
