Town guide

Mazunte

Mazunte is not Huatulco; it is a slower Oaxaca coast village that works as a day trip or overnight add-on when you want turtle-center context, Punta Cometa sunsets, and a different beach-town rhythm.

Quick verdict

How to use Mazunte

Best for: day-trippers with an early start, turtle/conservation interest, Punta Cometa, San Agustinillo pairing, and travelers considering an overnight coast extension. Avoid as a rushed late-afternoon add-on from Huatulco.

Best fit

  • Choose it when the page purpose matches your day, not because it is another dot on the map.
  • Build transport before dinner or sunset; return taxis are easier from town centers than remote beaches.
  • Keep cash and offline maps handy once you leave the main Huatulco bay loop.

How to plan it

Leave early, pick one anchor such as the Mexican Turtle Center area or Punta Cometa, and keep the day simple. Roads and coastal stops make the trip feel longer than a map glance suggests, so do not stack Mazunte, Zipolite, Puerto Ángel, and multiple beaches unless you have a driver and a long day.

Pair it with

Pair with San Agustinillo for a gentler beach stop, Zipolite only if its open-coast identity genuinely fits your group, or Puerto Ángel for a fishing-port contrast on the way back.

Common mistake

Trying to visit every town in one day. The Oaxaca coast rewards fewer stops, earlier starts, and enough time for meals, heat, road curves, and a relaxed return.

Mini route for a first visit

Start

Arrive with one clear anchor: meal, market, boat departure, archaeology, turtle-center context, or sunset.

Middle

Add one nearby stop that makes geographic sense instead of zig-zagging across the coast.

Finish

Return before the group is tired; save the second town or beach for another morning.
