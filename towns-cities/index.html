Town planning

Quick verdict: use each town for a specific job

Huatulco planning gets easier when each town has a role: La Crucecita for meals/errands, Santa Cruz for boats, Copalita/Bocana for river-mouth and archaeology context, Mazunte and Puerto Ángel for longer coast days.

Choose / avoid

  • Choose this when: you are deciding where to spend a morning, evening, or coast day and need practical route logic.
  • Avoid this when: you expect every nearby place to be a quick filler stop; Mazunte and Puerto Ángel need real drive-time and return planning.
  • Better backup idea: If a west-coast day feels too long, keep the day local: La Crucecita + Chahué, Santa Cruz + boat/lunch, or Copalita/Bocana only.

Choose by what you want to do

La Crucecita solves food and errands. Santa Cruz solves boat logistics. Copalita adds a river-mouth beach and archaeology context. Mazunte and Puerto Ángel are longer Oaxaca coast outings, planned coastal outings, not quick filler stops.

La Crucecita

Your practical center for dinner, errands, markets, banks, cafés, and a first-night town walk. Best paired with Chahué or Santa Cruz.

Santa Cruz

Use it for boat-tour departures, waterfront meals, easy swimming, services for cruise visitors and boat-tour days, and a simple marina-facing half day.

Copalita / Bocana

Mazunte

Puerto Ángel

A working fishing-port contrast to the resort bays. Better as one stop on a coastal route than as a polished beach-club day.

Simple route logic

  • Short evening: La Crucecita dinner, zócalo, mezcal or coffee, then taxi back.
  • Boat day: Santa Cruz start, bay route, late lunch, then La Crucecita if you still have energy.
  • West-side culture: Copalita/Bocana first, then choose either Chahué/Tangolunda return or a longer Mazunte direction.
  • Full coast day: Mazunte, San Agustinillo, and possibly Puerto Ángel with an early start and planned return.
