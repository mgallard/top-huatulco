The nine bays

Pick the right water for the day

Huatulco is not one beach. It is a cluster of protected bays. Some are easy resort beaches, some are town beaches, and the best snorkeling spots often need a boat.

Tangolunda

largest resort bay. Best for first-timers, families, resort beach days. Long protected bay with the easiest swimming, hotels, golf course access, restaurants, and beach clubs.

Open guide
Chahué

walkable town beach. Best for sunsets, local meals, easy evenings. Wide public beach, palapas, lifeguard presence in busy periods, and restaurants behind the sand.

Open guide
Santa Cruz

harbor and boat hub. Best for boat tours, seafood, cruise arrivals. Working marina, small beach, seafood stands, and the easiest place to arrange bay tours.

Open guide
El Maguey

small snorkel bay. Best for calm water, casual lunch, short boat trips. Sheltered water, palapa restaurants, and easy snorkeling near rocks.

Open guide
Órgano

quiet neighboring bay. Best for low-key swimming, fewer vendors. Smaller, quieter, less developed bay near Maguey with clearer water when conditions are calm.

Open guide
Cacaluta

remote national park bay. Best for scenery, clear water, boat-day feel. Large undeveloped bay inside the national park with striking open scenery and no resort feel.

Open guide
Chachacual

protected snorkel stop. Best for snorkeling, clear water, boat tours. Calm clear water, limited shade, national-park setting, and strong snorkel appeal.

Open guide
San Agustín

longer local beach. Best for beach walking, local weekends, relaxed lunch. Long beach with local restaurants, more open water, and a less polished feel.

Open guide
Conejos

small upscale bay. Best for quiet resort edge, rocky viewpoints. Smaller bay with upscale lodging nearby, rocky edges, and calm pockets for swimming.
