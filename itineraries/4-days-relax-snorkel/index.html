4 Days in Huatulco for Relaxing and Snorkeling

A practical four-day route for travelers who want calm water, a boat day, town food, and enough downtime to avoid over-scheduling Huatulco.

On this page

Who this route fits

Best for couples, families, and first-timers who want Huatulco to feel easy: one arrival day, one true water day, one flexible town/culture day, and one final bay day. Choose Santa Cruz for boat access, Chahué for central movement, or Tangolunda for resort calm.

Who should avoid it

Avoid this plan if your priority is surfing, nightlife, or a long west-coast loop. This route intentionally keeps Mazunte and Puerto Ángel out unless you add a fifth day.

Day-by-day plan

Each day has a job. Keep the route clustered so the trip feels like Huatulco, not a series of transfers.

Day 1

Arrive and choose your easiest water

Morning: Check in, unpack, and use Santa Cruz, Chahué, or your resort beach as the easy first swim/look-around.

Afternoon: Do a short grocery/cash run in La Crucecita if your hotel location makes that practical.

Evening: Eat in town or near your base, then confirm tomorrow's snorkel/boat conditions.

Getting around: Pre-arrange airport transfer or taxi; do not negotiate your whole trip plan tired at arrival.

Day 2

Protected bay snorkeling or boat day

Morning: Use the morning for your best water conditions. Pick a Santa Cruz boat route or Maguey-style easy snorkel depending on your group.

Afternoon: Take the long lunch/swim stop seriously; this is the day to be in the water, not in taxis.

Evening: Keep dinner simple after sun exposure. La Crucecita works if everyone still has energy.

Getting around: Ask about snorkel gear, shade, lunch, fees, and return time before paying.

Day 3

Town, market, Copalita, or recovery day

Morning: If everyone wants a dry day, go to Mercado 3 de Mayo in La Crucecita or Copalita early. If the water was poor yesterday, use this as your backup snorkel morning.

Afternoon: Return to a hotel pool, Chahué, or Santa Cruz. Avoid stacking another long excursion unless the group wants it.

Evening: Plan a better dinner: Terra-Cotta, El Sabor de Oaxaca, Mercader, or a bay seafood spot depending on your base.

Getting around: Use taxis for La Crucecita; arrange wait/return for Copalita.

Day 4

Final bay and departure-friendly plan

Morning: Choose the easiest bay near your base: Maguey for beach lunch, Santa Cruz for logistics, or Tangolunda for resort calm.

Afternoon: Pack before lunch if departing. If staying overnight, this can be your second water attempt.

Evening: One last town walk or early night before travel.

Getting around: Do not put Cacaluta, Chachacual, or a west-coast drive on departure day.

Departure timing is tightUse Santa Cruz, Chahué, Tangolunda, or La Crucecita. Do not choose remote beaches or west-coast drives.
