Huatulco itinerary

5 Days Oaxaca Coast Explorer

A deeper route for travelers who want Huatulco plus Mazunte, Puerto Ángel, Copalita, and a second bay day.

Quick verdict

Who this route fits

Choose it

First-timers who want beaches, one boat day, town food, and enough flexibility for heat and sea conditions.

Avoid it

Travelers trying to cover the whole Oaxaca coast in one rush. Add nights if Mazunte, Puerto Escondido, or mountain roads are major goals.

Pair it with

A base decision: Tangolunda for resorts, La Crucecita for food/logistics, Santa Cruz for boat access, Chahué for easy evenings.

Before the day-by-day plan

Choose the base before choosing the beaches

If you want the easiest trip

Stay in Tangolunda or near Chahué, use taxis, and keep one day centered on Santa Cruz boat logistics. This reduces friction for families, older travelers, and anyone arriving tired.

If you want more local evenings

Base around La Crucecita or Chahué, then use taxis to beaches. This makes dinners, markets, ATMs, and errands easier, but you will plan beach transport more deliberately.

If you want remote-feeling bays

Do not chase them on arrival day. Put the boat or west-bay day in the middle of the trip, when you can check weather, compare operators, and leave early.

If conditions change

Move the boat day, not the whole vacation. Huatulco has enough town, food, and easy-bay options that a windy morning should become a smarter plan, not a bad trip.

Day 1

Arrival and central bay orientation

Morning

Settle into Tangolunda, Chahué, Santa Cruz, or La Crucecita. Keep the first day close: beach walk, cash, groceries, taxi timing, and a simple dinner.

Afternoon / evening

Keep the afternoon close to your base: a short beach walk, groceries, cash, and a low-pressure meal. Save long transfers and boat decisions until you have local timing in your head.

Backup idea

If you arrive tired, skip sightseeing pressure. Huatulco heat and sun make an easy first evening smarter than a forced checklist.

Day 2

Nine-bay boat route

Morning

Use Santa Cruz as the practical launch point. Prioritize two or three quality stops over a rushed “see everything” loop. Chachacual, Cacaluta, Órgano, and Maguey each serve different needs.

Afternoon / evening

Let the sea conditions choose the afternoon rhythm. If visibility is good, give snorkeling real time; if wind builds, switch toward a sheltered lunch stop and return before everyone is tired.

Backup idea

Choose the operator for safety and timing, not only price. Confirm protected-area guidance and whether wildlife/coral rules are explained.

Day 3

La Crucecita and Copalita context day

Morning

Spend the morning in La Crucecita for market, church, coffee, and errands. Use the afternoon for Bocana del Río Copalita archaeology/nature context or a sunset at Chahué.

Afternoon / evening

After the town morning, use the warmer hours for Copalita, a shaded meal, or hotel downtime. Sunset works well at Chahué because it is easy to reach and easy to leave.

Backup idea

This day gives the trip cultural shape so Huatulco does not become only beaches and boat photos.

Day 4

Mazunte, San Agustinillo, or Puerto Ángel day trip

Morning

Pick one coast direction, not all of them. Mazunte is better for turtle-center/coast-town atmosphere; Puerto Ángel is better for a working-port contrast; San Agustinillo works for a slower beach meal.

Afternoon / evening

Make the afternoon about one coast town, not a road-race. Eat slowly, leave before dark if you are not using a driver, and do not add extra stops just because they look close on the map.

Backup idea

Road time matters. Leave early, avoid night driving if you are not comfortable, and confirm return transport before going far west.

Day 5

Second bay day matched to what you missed

Morning

If Day 2 was remote and wild, use El Maguey or Tangolunda for an easy serviced day. If Day 2 was basic, use San Agustín or a calmer snorkeling cove if conditions are right.

Afternoon / evening

Choose the afternoon based on what the trip still lacks: a serviced lunch, one more swim, a quieter cove, or a relaxed final sunset. Do not let the last day become a packing-stress sprint.

Backup idea

Make the final day about the best available conditions, not a fixed list. Huatulco changes by wind, swell, crowd timing, and group energy.

Practical questions

Should I rent a car for this itinerary?
Not for the central bay plan. Taxis and boats can handle most first-trip days. A car or driver becomes more useful for Copalita, Mazunte, Puerto Ángel, or a flexible west along the Oaxaca coast day.
Where should I stay?
Choose Tangolunda for resort ease, La Crucecita for restaurants and errands, Santa Cruz for boat logistics, or Chahué for a middle ground. The best base depends on whether beach access or evening food matters more.
What can go wrong?
The usual problems are overpacking the route, ignoring heat, choosing the cheapest boat without checking inclusions, and forgetting return transport from less central beaches.
