Itinerary

5 Days in Huatulco and the Oaxaca Coast

A five-day route that adds one wider Oaxaca coast day without sacrificing Huatulco's bays, town food, Copalita, and easy beach recovery time.

On this page

Who this route fits

Best for travelers who want Huatulco as the base but still want one look west toward Mazunte, San Agustinillo, or Puerto Ángel. The key is to protect the boat day and treat the west-coast loop as one focused day, not three rushed towns.

Who should avoid it

Avoid if your group hates long drives or if water days are the only priority. Use the four-day relax/snorkel route instead and keep the extra day for weather backup.

Day-by-day plan

Each day has a job. Keep the route clustered so the trip feels like Huatulco, not a series of transfers.

Day 1

Arrival and central bay orientation

Morning: Settle near Santa Cruz, Chahué, or Tangolunda and do the easiest nearby beach first.

Afternoon: Use La Crucecita for cash, snacks, and a simple first town dinner.

Evening: Confirm boat/weather plans for Day 2 or 3.

Getting around: Airport transfer first; short taxis only after check-in.

Day 2

Nine-bay style boat route

Morning: Use the best morning for the classic bay route from Santa Cruz.

Afternoon: Swim/snorkel where conditions are actually good, not where the brochure promised.

Evening: Eat near your base and rest.

Getting around: Choose an operator with clear pickup, lunch, gear, and fee terms.

Day 3

Copalita and town context

Morning: Visit Copalita early, or use La Crucecita market if the site/status does not fit your group.

Afternoon: Pair with Bocana/Tangolunda lunch or a resort pool afternoon.

Evening: Use this as your nicer dinner night in town.

Getting around: Agree on taxi wait/pickup for Copalita before entering.

Day 4

Mazunte, San Agustinillo, or Puerto Ángel

Morning: Leave early and choose one main west-coast anchor. Mazunte works best when the town and turtle-center context are the focus.

Afternoon: Add San Agustinillo or Puerto Ángel only if the day still feels relaxed.

Evening: Return before everyone is exhausted; dinner should be simple.

Getting around: Private driver or reputable tour is easier than improvising the return.

Day 5

Easy final beach or backup water day

Morning: Use Maguey, Santa Cruz, Chahué, or your resort beach depending on departure time.

Afternoon: If a previous water day failed, this is your backup window.

Evening: Pack, settle bills, and keep the airport plan boring.

Getting around: No remote beaches on departure day unless you are staying another night.
