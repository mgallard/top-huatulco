Huatulco itinerary

3 Days in Huatulco

A balanced first-trip route: easy bay, boat day, La Crucecita, and one coast/nature choice without rushing.

Quick verdict

Who this route fits

Choose it

First-timers who want beaches, one boat day, town food, and enough flexibility for heat and sea conditions.

Avoid it

Travelers trying to cover the whole Oaxaca coast in one rush. Add nights if Mazunte, Puerto Escondido, or mountain roads are major goals.

Pair it with

A base decision: Tangolunda for resorts, La Crucecita for food/logistics, Santa Cruz for boat access, Chahué for easy evenings.

Before the day-by-day plan

Choose the base before choosing the beaches

If you want the easiest trip

Stay in Tangolunda or near Chahué, use taxis, and keep one day centered on Santa Cruz boat logistics. This reduces friction for families, older travelers, and anyone arriving tired.

If you want more local evenings

Base around La Crucecita or Chahué, then use taxis to beaches. This makes dinners, markets, ATMs, and errands easier, but you will plan beach transport more deliberately.

If you want remote-feeling bays

Do not chase them on arrival day. Put the boat or west-bay day in the middle of the trip, when you can check weather, compare operators, and leave early.

If conditions change

Move the boat day, not the whole vacation. Huatulco has enough town, food, and easy-bay options that a windy morning should become a smarter plan, not a bad trip.

Day 1

Arrive, settle, and choose an easy bay

Morning

Use Tangolunda if you want resort simplicity, Chahué for a public beach and sunset, or Santa Cruz if your priority is arranging the boat day. Keep dinner in La Crucecita so the first night is simple.

Afternoon / evening

Use the afternoon for a gentle swim or beach walk only if conditions look calm. Keep the evening in La Crucecita or near your hotel so nobody is navigating unfamiliar roads tired.

Backup idea

Do not book a remote boat tour for arrival day. Use the afternoon to learn taxi timing, buy water, get cash, and ask about current sea conditions.

Day 2

Boat day through the protected bays

Morning

Start from Santa Cruz and choose a responsible operator for Chachacual, Cacaluta, Órgano, or Maguey depending on conditions. Ask what is included: park fee, snorkel gear, shade time, drinking water, and return hour.

Afternoon / evening

Ask the captain where the water is calmest that day, then spend the afternoon at the best stop instead of demanding every bay. Return early enough for showers and an easy dinner.

Backup idea

If wind or swell makes the route poor, switch to El Maguey or Santa Cruz beach time and save the remote bays for the calmer window.

Day 3

La Crucecita, Copalita, or one more beach

Morning

Use the final day for La Crucecita market/church/food, Copalita archaeology if you want culture, or San Agustín if the group wants a bigger west-bay beach day.

Afternoon / evening

Use the afternoon for one final low-friction choice: market browsing, Copalita, Chahué sunset, or a serviced beach lunch. Protect departure timing and avoid a late remote-beach return.

Backup idea

Do not cram Mazunte or Puerto Ángel into the last day unless your departure is late and you enjoy road time. Huatulco rewards slower pacing.

Practical questions

Should I rent a car for this itinerary?
Not for the central bay plan. Taxis and boats can handle most first-trip days. A car or driver becomes more useful for Copalita, Mazunte, Puerto Ángel, or a flexible west-coast day.
Where should I stay?
Choose Tangolunda for resort ease, La Crucecita for restaurants and errands, Santa Cruz for boat logistics, or Chahué for a middle ground. The best base depends on whether beach access or evening food matters more.
What can go wrong?
The usual problems are overpacking the route, ignoring heat, choosing the cheapest boat without checking inclusions, and forgetting return transport from less central beaches.
